## Vector DB Use Case
A traditional keyword-based database search would not suffice for this legal contract search system. Keyword search requires exact or near-exact term matches, creating a critical semantic gap—when lawyers ask "What are the termination clauses?", they won't find relevant sections using terms like "contract dissolution," "agreement cancellation," or "early exit provisions." Legal documents employ varied terminology where synonyms and related concepts are common, yet keyword search remains context-blind, unable to distinguish whether "termination" refers to contract termination, employee termination, or service termination across 500 pages.
A vector database plays an essential role by converting both contract text and natural language questions into high-dimensional vector embeddings that capture semantic meaning rather than just words. The system splits contracts into chunks (paragraphs or sections), generates embeddings for each chunk using legal-domain models, and stores them in the vector database with metadata like page numbers and clause types. When lawyers query in plain English, the system converts their question to an embedding and uses cosine similarity to retrieve the most semantically relevant sections, regardless of exact wording. This enables finding "force majeure" clauses when asked about "unforeseeable circumstances" and understanding that "What are my obligations?" should retrieve sections on duties, responsibilities, and deliverables. Vector databases thus bridge the gap between human language and legal document content, enabling accurate, context-aware semantic search at scale.
